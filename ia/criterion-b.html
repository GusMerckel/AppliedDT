Criterion B — Conceptual Design

Criterion B is about generating and developing design ideas. You need to show a range of creative ideas, then develop the most promising one into a detailed design solution. Maximum 8 marks.

On this page

What

What the examiner is looking for

Key points

  • A range of genuinely different initial ideas (not variations of one idea)
  • Clear annotation — drawings that explain how and why, not just what
  • Justified selection of the most promising idea
  • Detailed development of the chosen idea with modelling evidence
