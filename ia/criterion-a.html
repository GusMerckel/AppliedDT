Criterion A — Identifying a Need

Criterion A asks you to clearly identify a real problem or opportunity, research the context, and establish a detailed design brief and specification that will guide your entire project. Maximum 8 marks.

On this page

What

What the examiner is looking for

Key points

  • A real, justified problem with evidence of user research
  • Clear identification of the client/end-user
  • Thorough analysis of existing products
  • A detailed, measurable design specification (not just a list of wishes)

Writing

Writing your Design Specification

Key points

  • Use measurable criteria — not 'it should be comfortable' but 'the handle diameter should be 30–40mm'
  • Organise by category: function, ergonomics, materials, aesthetics, sustainability, safety
  • Each criterion should be testable — you'll return to this in Criterion D
