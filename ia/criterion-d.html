Criterion D — Testing & Evaluation

Criterion D asks you to rigorously test your prototype against your design specification and evaluate how well it meets the needs of your client. Maximum 6 marks.

On this page

What

What the examiner is looking for

Key points

  • Tests that directly match the criteria in your design specification
  • Evidence of testing — results, photographs, user feedback
  • Honest, critical evaluation — not just 'it works great'
  • Justified and realistic recommendations for improvement

Writing

Writing a strong evaluation

Key points

  • Go through each specification criterion one by one
  • Use evidence (measurements, user quotes, photos) to justify your judgement
  • Be honest about what didn't work — examiners reward critical thinking
  • Suggest specific, realistic improvements
